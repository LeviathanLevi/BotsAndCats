#1 Mean Reversion

This strategy trades on the assumption that the price will return back to the mean or average price.

Mean reversion works off a simple concept, regression to the mean. Regression to the mean is a phenomon that states that when a sample point is an extreme outlier the future points will be closer to the mean/average.

Take a look at the photo on the right, the blue line is a rough estimate of the mean of these prices. When a commodity's value strays from that average you could make a bet that it will likely return to the mean at some point in the future.

While there are many ways to trade with mean reversion so the most simplist you can probably imagine is to buy the commodity when it drops significantly below the mean, and sell when it rises significantly above the mean.
